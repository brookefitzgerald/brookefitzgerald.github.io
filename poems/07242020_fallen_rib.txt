I saw a fallen tree today.

Half of its limbs had broken, the rest curled into a coniferous rib cage.

I saw in that tree the fossilized ribs of a wooly mammoth - a reminder of humans' irreversible effects on the ecosystem, mammoths having been hunted to extinction before the anthropocene.

I saw in that tree the rib that God pulled from Adam to make Eve - a reminder of the Christian mythos of women as secondary and as property, a mythos that justified putting women into ribbed cages of immanence and objectification.

I saw in that tree the ribs protecting the lungs of the Earth - a reminder that trees everywhere convert carbon dioxide to oxygen in a continuous planetary breath, a breath daily shortened by fires and deforestation.

Then I saw in that tree a squirrel - a reminder that after all this that tree is a tree.
